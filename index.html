Research Experience

Radiation Oncology AI Research Assistant under Dr. Samuel Luk

Boston University Chobanian & Avedisian School of Medicine | Sep. 2024 – Present

Project 1: Multi-Institutional Study on Clinical Practice Differences Impacting a Bayesian-based AI Quality Assurance (QA) Model in Radiation Oncology.

• Developed a Python package using NLP, vector space models, and differential privacy to quantify oncology treatment differences across clinics, enabling secure international inter-clinic analysis of 2M+ cases.

• Engineered a cosine similarity-based comparison framework, achieving >90% alignment with ground truth and revealing significant inter-clinic variations in tumor location-based practices (e.g., dose, MU).

• Implemented statistical testing in R to correlate quantified differences with Error Detection Bayesian Network performance, contributing to 20-25% improvement in model generalizability across clinics.

• Built end-to-end analysis pipeline producing shareable outputs and comparsion plots used in multi-institutional collaboration, improving clinical QA and promoting adoption of AI safety tools in oncology.

• Project 1 Repo: shaotaihu/VSM **Presented at Boston University 2025 UROP Symposium & WIP publication**

Project 2: Improve Quality and Efficiency of Pretreatment Physics Plan Review Using Explainable Deep Learning Model.

• Designing an explainable hybrid deep learning model for treatment plan review in radiation oncology with latent spaces from autoencoders and discriminators from generative adversarial networks.

• Building the pipeline using PyTorch, TensorFlow, and Scikit-learn, tuning parameters using validation versus training loss and prioritizing recall over precision.

• Aiming to achieve a scalable alternative to Bayesian QA models by deploying deep neural networks with traceable latent space workflows, contributing to safer, more efficient radiation oncology practices.

Boston University Genetics & Genomics | Sep. 2024 – Present
