Research Experience

Radiation Oncology Data and AI Research Assistant under Dr. Samuel Luk

Boston University Chobanian & Avedisian School of Medicine | Sep. 2024 – Present

Project 1: Multi-Institutional Study on Clinical Practice Differences Impacting a Bayesian-Based Quality Assurance (QA) AI Model in Radiation Oncology with Machine Learning (ML).

• Developed a Python package using NLP, vector space models, and differential privacy to quantify treatment differences across clinics, enabling secure international inter-clinic comparison of 2M+ cases.

• Engineered a ML and cosine similarity comparison framework, achieving >90% alignment with ground truth and revealing significant inter-clinic variations based on tumor location.

• Conducted statistical tests (t-test, KS test) in R to correlate quantified differences with QA AI model performance, improving model generalizability by 20-25% across clinics.

• Built end-to-end pipeline producing shareable outputs and comparison plots for multi-institutional collaboration via GitHub, improving clinical QA and promoting AI QA tools in oncology.

• Project 1 Repo: shaotaihu/vsm *WIP publication* *Received Spring 2025 SRA $1500*

Project 2: Improve Quality and Efficiency of Pretreatment Physics Plan Review Using Explainable Deep Learning AI Model.

• Developing an explainable treatment plan error detection AI using Autoencoders and GANs to improve treatment personalization and safety in radiation oncology.

• Building pipeline with PyTorch, TensorFlow, and Scikit-learn, current model achieved 92% recall and 90% F1, indicating the model’s powerful potential in detecting erroneous treatment plans.

• Aiming for a big data scalable alternative to Bayesian QA AI through deep learning AI models with explainable workflows, contributing to safer and individualized radiotherapy.

• Project 2 Repo: shaotaihu/deeplearning *Received Fall 2025 SRA $750*
